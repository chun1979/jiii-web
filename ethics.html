Statement on Publication Ethics

Adhering to the highest standards of integrity in scholarly research.

Intelligence, Informatics and Infrastructure is committed to maintaining the highest ethical standards. We strictly follow the guidelines recommended by the COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics).

1 Obligations of Editors

The primary responsibility of a journal editor is to ensure an efficient and fair review process of manuscripts submitted for publication, and to establish and maintain high standards of technical and professional quality.

  • Impartiality: Editors should limit themselves only to the intellectual content, ensuring fair evaluation without regard to race, gender, sexual orientation, religious belief, ethnic origin, citizenship, or political philosophy.
  • Confidentiality: Editors must ensure the confidentiality of the submitted works until they are published.
  • Conflict of Interest: An editor who authors or coauthors a manuscript submitted for consideration to the journal shall not review that work.

2 Obligations of Reviewers

The reviewers’ role is to fairly determine whether the submitted manuscript is eligible for publication.

  • Confidentiality: Reviewers are bound to treat the manuscript received for peer reviewing as confidential.
  • No Personal Advantage: Reviewers must not use the information obtained through peer review for personal advantage.
  • Conflict of Interest: Reviewers should not consider manuscripts in which they have conflicts of interest with any of the authors, companies, or institutions.

3 Obligations of Authors

An author’s central obligation is to present a concise account of their research completed with an objective discussion of its significance.

  • Originality: Authors should ensure that they have written entirely original works. Any attempt of plagiarism will result in rejection.
  • No Multiple Submissions: Submitting the same manuscript to more than one journal concurrently constitutes unethical publishing behavior.
  • Citation: When the authors use other materials, sources should be appropriately cited.

4 Policy on Generative AI

Authors are allowed to use generative AI technologies to improve readability and language, but strict conditions apply:

  • Disclosure: Authors must disclose the use of such technologies in the manuscript.
  • Responsibility: Authors are ultimately responsible for the content produced or improved by AI.
  • Images: Generative AI is not permitted for creating or altering images, except when it is part of the research design itself.

For detailed terms, please refer to relevant policies such as Elsevier's AI Policies.
